History of Detective Conan

was first published as a manga series in January 1994 by Gosho Aoyama in Weekly Shōnen Sunday. The story centers around Shinichi Kudo, a high school detective who is transformed into a child after being poisoned by a mysterious organization. Adopting the alias Conan Edogawa, he continues solving cases while secretly investigating the organization that altered his life.

The anime adaptation premiered in January 1996, produced by TMS Entertainment and broadcast on Yomiuri TV. Initially expected to be a short series, its popularity soared due to its clever mysteries, engaging characters, and emotional depth. Over time, Detective Conan evolved into one of the longest-running anime series in history.

Anime Series

Since its debut in 1996, Detective Conan has grown into one of the longest-running anime series in history, surpassing 1,000 episodes and continuing to captivate audiences across generations. Created by Gosho Aoyama, the series follows the brilliant teenage detective Shinichi Kudo, who is transformed into a child and adopts the alias Conan Edogawa. Despite his new form, Conan relentlessly solves cases while searching for the truth behind the mysterious organization that changed his life.

Reaching 1,000 episodes is more than a numerical milestone—it’s a testament to the series’ enduring appeal, clever storytelling, and emotional depth. Each episode presents a new mystery, a new challenge, and a new opportunity to explore the complexities of human behavior, logic, and justice.

Movies

Since 1997, Detective Conan has released a new theatrical movie almost every year, expanding the universe beyond the TV series. These films feature high-stakes cases, international settings, and deeper character development—often focusing on major confrontations with the Black Organization, rival detectives, or large-scale mysteries.

With over 25 feature films, the movie series has become a beloved tradition among fans, known for its cinematic quality, emotional depth, and thrilling plots. Each movie stands alone, but often ties into the larger narrative of Conan’s journey to reclaim his true identity.

Faction

The world of Detective Conan is filled with mystery—not just in its cases, but also in the complex web of organizations and factions that shape the story. Each group has its own motives, secrets, and key characters that influence the overarching narrative. Here are the major factions in the series:

Black Organization

A secretive and dangerous criminal syndicate responsible for many of the series’ darkest events. They are behind the development of the poison APTX 4869, which transformed Shinichi Kudo into Conan Edogawa. Known for their codenames based on alcoholic drinks (e.g., Gin, Vodka, Vermouth), their true goals remain shrouded in mystery.

FBI

An American law enforcement agency operating in Japan to investigate and dismantle the Black Organization. The FBI agents often collaborate with Conan and have their own vendettas against the syndicate.

CIA

Another intelligence agency working undercover within the Black Organization. Their agents risk their lives to gather intel from the inside.

Notable Agents: Hidemi Hondou (a.k.a. Rena Mizunashi / Kir)

Japanese Public Security Bureau

Japan’s domestic intelligence agency, also working to infiltrate and monitor the Black Organization. They often operate independently from the FBI and CIA.
